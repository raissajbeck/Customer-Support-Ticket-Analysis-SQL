# 📊 Customer Support Ticket Analysis Using SQL

## Project Overview

This project analyzes customer support ticket data using SQL to uncover trends in ticket volume, ticket status, priority levels, support channels, customer demographics, and customer satisfaction. The objective is to transform raw support data into meaningful business insights that can help organizations improve customer service operations and decision-making.

---

## Business Problem

Customer support teams handle thousands of inquiries across multiple channels every day. Without analyzing support data, it is difficult to identify recurring issues, prioritize resources effectively, and improve the customer experience.

This project answers the following business questions:

- How many support tickets were received?
- Which ticket types are most common?
- Which priorities require the most attention?
- Which communication channels do customers use most?
- How satisfied are customers with the support they receive?

# Key Findings

## 1. Pending Customer Responses Are the Largest Ticket Group

The highest number of tickets (**2,881**) are waiting for customers to respond.

**Business Impact**

- Delayed customer responses can increase ticket resolution times and reduce operational efficiency.

---

## 2. Refund Requests Are the Most Common Issue

Refund requests represent the largest category of support tickets.

**Business Impact**

- This may indicate opportunities to improve product quality, order fulfillment, or customer expectations.

---

## 3. Customer Satisfaction Has Room for Improvement

The average customer satisfaction score is **2.99 out of 5**, suggesting that the support experience could be enhanced.

**Business Impact**

- Improving response times and first-contact resolution may help increase customer satisfaction.

---

## 4. Email Is the Most Frequently Used Support Channel

Email is the most common way customers contact support.

**Business Impact**

- Investing in email automation and response templates could improve efficiency and reduce response times.

---

## 5. Ticket Priorities Are Evenly Distributed

Medium, High, Critical, and Low priority tickets appear in relatively similar numbers.

**Business Impact**

- Support teams should maintain balanced staffing and ensure processes are in place to manage varying ticket priorities effectively.

---

# Business Recommendations

Based on this analysis, organizations should consider:

- Implementing automated reminders for customers with pending responses.
- Investigating the causes of frequent refund requests.
- Improving customer satisfaction through faster response and resolution times.
- Enhancing email support workflows with automation and standardized templates.
- Regularly reviewing ticket prioritization to ensure urgent issues receive timely attention.
